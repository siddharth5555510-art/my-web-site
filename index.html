🌱 Mechanistic Evaluation of Polyherbal Combination (Spine Guard, Gotu Kola, Brahmi)
Detailed Introduction, Phytochemical Constituents, Nephrotoxicity & Extraction

📖 Detailed Introduction

Acute nephritis represents a severe inflammatory condition of the renal parenchyma, often triggered by bacterial endotoxins such as lipopolysaccharide (LPS). LPS activates Toll-like receptor 4 (TLR4), initiating a cascade that converges on nuclear factor-kappa B (NF-κB) and the NOD-like receptor protein 3 (NLRP3) inflammasome. This results in excessive production of pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-1β, IL-18, TNF-α) and reactive oxygen species (ROS), leading to tubular necrosis and impaired renal function. Current therapies (corticosteroids, immunosuppressants) carry significant adverse effects, necessitating safer multi-targeted alternatives.

The defined polyherbal combination of Spine Guard (Tribulus terrestris), Gotu Kola (Centella asiatica), and Brahmi (Bacopa monnieri) is rooted in traditional Ayurvedic and adaptogenic principles. Each herb contributes unique phytochemicals that synergistically inhibit NF-κB translocation, disrupt NLRP3/ASC assembly, and restore endogenous antioxidant networks (SOD, catalase, glutathione). This thesis investigates their hydroalcoholic/aqueous-alcoholic extracts to maximize bioavailability of saponins, triterpenoids, and bacosides, aiming to validate a nephroprotective mechanism against LPS-induced acute nephritis.

⚠️ Nephrotoxicity & LPS Model Rationale

LPS-Induced Acute Nephritis: Intraperitoneal LPS (10 mg/kg) in rodents mimics gram-negative sepsis-associated kidney injury. It elevates serum creatinine, BUN, and triggers mitochondrial oxidative damage. Key features include NF-κB p65 nuclear translocation, NLRP3 upregulation, and caspase-1 cleavage. This model is ideal to evaluate reno-protective herbs that target both inflammatory and oxidative cascades.

Clinical relevance: Drug-induced and septic nephrotoxicity share common pathways (TLR4/MyD88/NF-κB). The polyherbal blend aims to counteract these without nephrotoxic burden, as the selected herbs have historical safety profiles and reported renal protective effects.

🍃 Gotu Kola (Centella asiatica (L.) Urban, Apiaceae)

🔬 Phytochemical Constituents

Triterpenoid Saponins: Asiaticoside, madecassoside, asiatic acid, madecassic acid (major bioactive markers).
Flavonoids: Quercetin, kaempferol, apigenin derivatives.
Volatile oils: β-caryophyllene, farnesene.
Other: Centellose, polyacetylenes, amino acids.

Key marker for standardization: Asiaticoside (≥2.5% w/w in extract) and madecassoside.

🧪 Extraction Method (Hydroalcoholic / Alcoholic)

Hydroalcoholic extraction (Ethanol 70% v/v): Dried aerial parts powdered (sieve #40). Maceration/percolation with ethanol-water (70:30) for 72 hours with intermittent shaking. Solvent ratio 1:8 (drug:solvent). Filtered, concentrated under reduced pressure (40–45°C) using rotary evaporator. Yield ~18–22% w/w. Lyophilized to powder.
Rationale: Asiaticoside and madecassoside are highly soluble in aqueous ethanol; hydroalcoholic mixture enhances triterpene extraction while preserving glycosidic bonds. Avoids high heat to prevent degradation.

Alternative alcoholic (95% ethanol): Used for enriched asiatic acid fraction; requires subsequent hydrolysis. For nephritis study, hydroalcoholic extract preferred due to balanced polarity.

🦴 Spine Guard (Tribulus terrestris L., Zygophyllaceae)

🔬 Phytochemical Constituents

Steroidal Saponins: Protodioscin, dioscin, tribulosin, terrestrosins A–K (furostanol and spirostanol types).
Flavonoids: Kaempferol, quercetin, rutin.
Alkaloids: Harmane, norharmane.
Other: Phytosterols (β-sitosterol), tannins.

Key marker for nephroprotection: Protodioscin (≥20% in standardized extract) – known to modulate NF-κB and ACE inhibition.

🧪 Extraction Method (Hydroalcoholic / Alcoholic)

Hydroalcoholic extraction (Methanol 50% or Ethanol 60%): Dried fruits/aerial parts coarsely ground. Reflux extraction (50°C) with ethanol:water (60:40) for 4 hours, repeated twice. Combined extracts filtered, concentrated under vacuum. Final extract dried to yield brownish powder (yield ~15–20%).
Rationale: Steroidal saponins like protodioscin require moderate polarity; hydroalcoholic solvent improves solubility. Avoids pure alcohol which may leave polar glycosides. Some protocols use 50% methanol for analytical fingerprinting; for in-vivo use, ethanol-water is safer.

Note: Aqueous-alcoholic maceration for 48h also employed; often standardized to 40% protodioscin by UV/HPLC.

🧠 Brahmi (Bacopa monnieri (L.) Wettst., Plantaginaceae)

🔬 Phytochemical Constituents

Triterpenoid Saponins (Bacosides): Bacoside A (mixture of bacoside A3, bacopaside II, etc.), bacoside B, bacopasaponins.
Alkaloids: Brahmine, herpestine, nicotine.
Flavonoids: Luteolin, apigenin glycosides.
Other: Betulinic acid, stigmasterol, cucurbitacins.

Key marker: Bacoside A content (≥20% in standardized extract) – responsible for antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

🧪 Extraction Method (Hydroalcoholic / Alcoholic)

Hydroalcoholic extraction (Ethanol 50–70%): Whole plant dried, powdered. Cold maceration or Soxhlet using ethanol:water (60:40 or 70:30) for 48–72 hours. Solvent ratio 1:6. Extract filtered, concentrated below 50°C under reduced pressure. Yield approx. 12–18%.
Rationale: Bacosides are amphiphilic; hydroalcoholic mixture efficiently extracts both aglycones and glycosidic saponins. High water content helps rupture plant matrix; ethanol ensures saponin solubility. Some pharmacopoeias recommend 50% ethanol for optimal bacoside A recovery.

Alcoholic extraction (90% ethanol): Used for defatting and subsequent bacoside enrichment, but may yield less polar fraction. Hydroalcoholic chosen for full-spectrum nephroprotective activity.

🛡️ Nephroprotective Synergy in LPS Nephritis

Spine Guard + Gotu Kola: Protodioscin and asiaticoside collectively inhibit IκB phosphorylation, blocking NF-κB p65 nuclear entry. This reduces priming of NLRP3.
Brahmi + Gotu Kola: Bacosides boost intracellular GSH and catalase, while madecassoside attenuates lipid peroxidation. Dual antioxidant defense preserves mitochondrial function.
All three together: Synergistic downregulation of NLRP3, ASC, caspase-1 cleavage → reduced IL-1β maturation and renal inflammation.

📘 All extraction procedures comply with standard pharmacopoeial guidelines; hydroalcoholic extracts were concentrated under vacuum and stored at −20°C until use. The defined combination is prepared in 1:1:1 ratio based on marker content for in-vivo LPS nephritis evaluation.
